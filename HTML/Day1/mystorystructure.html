My Voice, My Way

This is just my personal perspective and not related to academics.

I have faced many misunderstandings because I don’t express myself instantly—I take time to connect with the environment before I speak. Many people don’t understand this and often think I have an attitude or that I am rude or stubborn, but that is not the truth. During conversations, when I stay silent for a moment, some people assume that I am not interested or that I don’t like them. Because of this, I have lost a few connections, as people chose to stay away from me. But in reality, that was never my intention. At the same time, I understand that speaking at the right moment is important, and I am working on improving that. I personally feel that silence is one of the most beautiful things, and not everyone can truly feel or understand it. For me, silence is not empty—it gives me space to think, observe, and understand things in my own way. Even if not everyone understands silence, judging or disliking someone because of it is not right. There were times when I felt confused about myself because of how others saw me, but slowly I started understanding my own nature. I am learning to express myself better while still being true to who I am. At the end of the day, everyone has their own way of expressing themselves, and this is mine.
